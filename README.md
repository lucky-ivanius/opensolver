### What is OpenSolver?

OpenSolver is an open-source toolkit that lets solvers **discover, aggregate, and fulfill** orders across chains and intent protocols — from a single interface.

### Why this exists

Intent protocols are proliferating. UniswapX, Across, CoW Protocol, 1inch Fusion — each with its own order format, its own fulfillment flow, across different chains and standards. Solvers maintain separate infrastructure per protocol.

OpenSolver provides a common layer:

- **Order Aggregation** — A single normalized order stream across multiple protocols and chains.
- **Workflow-Aware** — Supports both open-competition orders and request-for-quote flow.
- **Transaction Builder** — A composable builder that constructs the right fulfillment transaction for the right protocol.
- **Modular by Design** — Every component — detectors, parsers, builders — is swappable and extendable.

### Who is this for?

- **Solvers** looking for a ready-made foundation to compete across intent protocols and chains
- **Teams** building solver infrastructure who want to skip the boilerplate
- **Researchers** exploring cross-protocol order flow and solver mechanics

### Status
